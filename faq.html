Why did Georgetown Univereity choose to develop a prize competition to address the issue of energy efficiency, and why did Bloomington/Monroe County join the competition?

Throughout history, highly visible prize competitions have demonstrated their capacity to spur innovative approaches to some of the most difficult challenges faced by mankind. Despite the many innovative efficiency measures now in place across the country - including incentives and tax breaks - the United States still wastes more than half of all energy produced. It's clear that we need a new approach to address this challenge, and the Georgetown University Energy Prize is positioned to act as the catalyst for the long-term, positive change Bloomington needs.

Of course Bloomington/Monroe County intends to win the award, but in the unlikey event that we do not, are there any rewards for finalist communities that aren't selected as the Prize winner?

Bloomington/Monroe County doesn't have to finish first in the competition to "win". Every competing community that demonstrates savings over previous years will be able to pass through a significant savings to their residents via energy bills for individual households - giving residents more disposable income, and allowing local governments to provide more money for direct services to residents. Moreover, the long-term benefits of energy efficiency include both financial and environmental benefits for future generations.

Back to top

How will this competition positively impact Bloomington/Monroe County?

In addition to the clear environmental benefits associated with becoming more energy efficient, communities that reduce their energy consumption ultimately reap substantial financial benefits as well including saving as much as $200 per year for the average household when implementing energy saving measures.

Back to top

What inspired Bloomington/Monroe County to enter the competition?

When it comes to energy efficiency, everyone wins. Consumers save money, municipal governments reduce their expenses, and our community becomes more energy independent and environmentally sustainable. The Monroe County Energy Challenge provides a unique platform that will bring together residents, government leaders and utilities in competing communities, united in the goal of improving their energy efficiency.
