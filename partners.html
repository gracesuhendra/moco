Our Partners



City of Bloomington

https://bloomington.in.gov/

The City of Bloomington, Indiana is located in Monroe County and is home to over 82,000 people. The City owns several facilities in addition to its offices, including water and wastewater treatment plants, recreational facilities, an ice skating rink, a performing arts theatre, and much more. Great strides have been taken to reduce energy consumption in these facilities over the past several years. The City of Bloomington Office of the Mayor is a strong supporter of the MoCo Energy Challenge.

Monroe County Government

www.co.monroe.in.us/

The Monroe County Government has actively championed energy conservation in the community over the past several years. In 2006 the Monroe County Council passed a resolution in support of an energy reduction goal of 3% per annum, which has been realized through several creative conservation initiatives. The Monroe County Government is a key member of the MoCo Energy Challenge by both taking actions to reduce its own energy consumption and by encouraging its residents to practice smart energy use habits.

Town of Stinesville

http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stinesville,_Indiana

The Town of Stinesville, Indiana is a small community in Monroe County that is home to 195 people. Stinesville has already shown its commitment to reducing its energy consumption by upgrading to LED lamps in some of its streetlights. Stinesville’s Town Council plans to pursue other conservation efforts during the MoCo Energy Challenge and to use the Challenge as an opportunity to reach out to and support conservation efforts among the town’s residents.

Town of Elletsville

http://ellettsville.in.us/

The Town of Ellettsville is home to 6,400 people, and the Town operates its own wastewater treatment plant, fire station, police station, and town hall. Ellettsville is an active participant in the MoCo Energy Challenge, and is committed to reducing its own energy use as well as reaching out to its residents to offer support and guidance in reducing the Town’s residential energy use.

*Siren: Southern Indiana Renewable Energy Network

http://www.sirensolar.org/

The Southern Indiana Renewable Energy Network (SIREN) is a volunteer powered, grassroots community group centered in Monroe County. Operating as a project under the Center for Sustainable Living, SIREN works to encourage the adoption of solar power across southern Indiana through research, education, outreach and consulting. SIREN routinely provides workshops on solar power and solar technology. SIREN is committed to promoting energy efficiency as a natural complement to solar power. IN this vein, SIREN has committed to promoting the Monroe County Energy Challenge by (1) giving the MCEC a high profile during our "Going Solar" workshops, (2) SIREN team members volunteering for all manner of MoCo Energy Challenge activities, and (3) pursuing SIREN's principle agenda of reducing the use of fossil fuel based power and accelerating the adoption of solar power throughout our community.

Utilities District of Western Indiana (UDWI) REMC

http://www.udwiremc.com/

The Utilities District of Western Indiana REMC, is a member of Touchstone Cooperatives. Touchstone co-ops are owned by the members they serve, and are committed to providing reliable electricity at the lowest price possible. UDWI REMC is working with the MoCo Energy Challenge to ensure residential, municipal, and K-12 electricity usage is accurately collected and reported to Georgetown University for the two year baseline period and the two year competition period.

Vectren

https://www.vectren.com/livesmart/landing.jsp

Vectren Corporation is a Fortune 1000 energy holding company headquartered in Evansville, Indiana. Through its utility subsidiaries (Vectren North, Vectren South, and Vectren Energy Delivery of Ohio), the company distributes natural gas to approximately one million business and residential customers in Indiana and Ohio. Vectren is working with the MoCo Energy Challenge to collect and report Monroe County natural gas usage to Georgetown University during the competition and is an active partner in promoting the challenge in the community.

South Central Community Action Program

www.insccap.org/

The South Central Community Action Program is supporting the Monroe County Energy Challenge through making energy efficiency improvements to homes occupied by low- income households and by providing energy efficiency education to clients who receive assistance in paying winter heating bills. SCCAP is also the owner of the new Monroe County EnergyMobile.

Earth Care Bloomington

https://bloomington.in.gov/documents/viewDocument.php?document_id=5769

Earth Care Bloomington, an affiliate of Hoosier Interfaith Power and Light, is charged with bringing Hoosiers of faith together to help curb global climate change. An Earth Care co-convener chairs the Monroe County Energy Challenge residential programs committee, and has adapted Earth Care’s Task of the Month program for use in the Energy Challenge. They promote Task of the Month and Energy Challenge opportunities through their monthly newsletter, and members share them with their congregations.

Community Foundation of Bloomington and Monroe County

www.cfbmc.org/

The Community Foundation fuels innovative ideas and lasting impact in Monroe County. They make grants, offer expertise, and lead in collaboration with others to meet our community’s most pressing needs and seize its most important opportunities. The Community Foundation is partnering with the MoCo Energy Challenge to house an energy fund made up of grant money and donations the Challenge receives.

South Central Indiana (SCI) REMC

www.sciremc.com/

SCI REMC is a not-for-profit member-focused cooperative providing safe, reliable, cost- effective electricity and energy solutions. In addition to SCI REMC’s commitment to helping its members use energy wisely, the cooperative is partnering with MoCo Energy Challenge to ensure electricity usage data is accurately collected and reported to Georgetown University during the competition.

Duke Energy

http://www.duke-energy.com/indiana.asp

Duke Energy is the largest electric power holding company in the United States, with assets also in Canada and Latin America, and is headquartered in Charlotte, North Carolina. Duke Energy is working closely with the MoCo Energy Challenge to ensure residential, municipal, and K-12 electricity usage is accurately collected and reported to Georgetown University for the two year baseline period and the two year competition period and is an active supporter of the Challenge.

Indiana University

http://www.iub.edu/

Although the Indiana University campus is not included in the Monroe County Energy Challenge, per the Georgetown University Energy Prize rules, Indiana University is supporting MCEC through involvement of IU Director of Sustainability on the MCEC Residential Committee and by acting as a conduit for communications to our faculty, staff and students, who make up a significant portion of the residential population of Monroe County. Although Indiana University campus is not officially part of the MCEC, it runs the largest single-campus energy challenge in the country twice a year. Indiana University is fully committed to energy efficiency in its buildings and operations, as described in the Bicentennial Strategic Plan.

Ivy Tech Community College

http://www.ivytech.edu/

Ivy Tech Community College prepares Indiana residents to learn, live, and work in a diverse and globally competitive environment by delivering professional, technical, transfer, and lifelong education. Through its affordable, open-access education and training programs, the College enhances the development of Indiana's citizens and communities and strengthens its economy. Supporting this overall mission, Ivy Tech Bloomington fosters the principles of service-learning and community service. In this vein Ivy Tech has committed to supporting the Monroe County Energy Challenge through a student powered and faculty directed design, development and management of the MCEC web presence. Ivy Tech Bloomington is proud to support the MoCo Energy Challenge goals of permanent community wide reductions in energy use and values highly the opportunity for Ivy Tech students to service the community in this capacity.
