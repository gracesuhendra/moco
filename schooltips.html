Schools play a vital part in the Energy Challenge. School buildings are big users of electricity and gas, paid for by taxpayers. Schools are also where our children learn about the world—and our world runs on electricity and gas. Understanding energy is an important part of their education. Angela Harding, Grade 2, Marlin Elementary does a “Lights Out” Exercise with her class. When there is enough natural light, she turns out the overhead light in the classroom and keeps track of how long they are out. Using a calculator, her class adds up the time and multiplies by the school’s cost of electricity to find out how much they are saving.
